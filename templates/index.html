What is American Sign Language (ASL)?

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language that serves as the primary means of communication for many deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals in North America. It has its own grammar, syntax, and cultural nuances, making it a rich and expressive language. ASL is not a visual representation of English—it is a unique language with its own rules and structure.

Learning ASL fosters inclusion and breaks down communication barriers, enabling stronger connections and accessibility for all. Whether you're looking to communicate with a loved one or simply expand your communication skills, ASL opens doors to meaningful interaction.
